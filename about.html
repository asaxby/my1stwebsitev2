I'm Astrid Saxby a UX / UI designer

I am not your usual applicant for this post as I am a career changer and because of this I offer you extra skills that other applicants might not have. It was a brave decision to leave the career I love in teaching to follow a new adventure, but I haven’t regretted the decision. I have just finished a year-long course on UX design with Career Foundary in UX design. During the course I developed an app to allow climbers to quickly access a specialised physio or to find a training plan to recover from injury. As part of my project have researched a range of user testing methods and conducted surveys with a range of climbers. I analysed the results and then developed user personas and a user flow for the app. Using the knowledge from my research I designed wireframes and animated prototypes to show the working of the app. I conducted more user testing and integrated the results into the final design. Feedback from fellow students and CF staff praised the clean, clear, professional appearance of the app and complimented its usability. So why did I want to leave the career I love to go into UX? Well, I am someone who loves a challenge and loves to learn new things. One of my favourite activities as a teacher was to come up with new resources which would help students understand a complex topic, creating visual, appealing and understandable resources for secondary students who might have a reading age of around 7 when their chronological age was 15 was one of the best parts of the job. One of my greatest skills is to be able to look at something complex and to develop a logical sequence through it. I have also redesigned a booking system for my climbers’ club huts and produced a prototype with 2 simple screens in which to input a range of complex information, this straight-forward app will automate a process which was previously done manually by a volunteer. Before teaching I worked as a Development Trainer running groupwork sessions. I ran training for up to 48 young people, organising accommodation, staffing, activities and training workshops during the week-long courses. I am someone who likes to be active, and my interests reflect this, I am an keen rock-climber and caver, I love to travel and have visited over 40 countries in the world, I’m always looking forward to the next adventure.
